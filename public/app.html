App

Settings

Bundesland

The first thing to do is to select your Bundesland. This way the app will show you the questions related to your Bundesland.

You can change your Bundesland at any time in the settings.


Settings

Change the Bundesland. Set how many questions to show in the Exercise Smart List. Choose how you prefer to see your questions: inline in the exam list or in a separate view for each question.

You can also set how many questions answer to remember for statistics and reset your statistics data.

Categories

Tests

Questions are organized in different categories by theme or topic. Or in smart lists.

You can navigate the catalog of all 300 generic questions or the 10 questions of your Bundesland.

You can simulate a test of 33 random questions or do an Exercise of 20 new questions every time.


Smart list

You can build your own exam list based on a keyword or by tags.

The App keeps track of your latest answers and builds smart lists so you can choose to train, for example, on only questions you got mostly wrong.


Exams

Exam

When you select a category or smart list, the app starts an Exam with a set of questions.

You can choose to respond to the questions inline, in the exam list, or to see each question in its own window.


Progress

For each exam, the app keeps track of the time (you have 60 minutes for each exam), and the questions answered, right or wrong.

The app gives each exam a score based on your right or wrong answers. This way you can see, for each category, where you are strong or not.


Result

When you answer all the questions in the exam, or When the time is out, you will see the result.

If you have answered right, more than the threshold of half of the questions, you will pass the exam!


Sort

You can sort the questions in the exam by the Question number (natural order).

Or by the rating of each question, so you can, for example, see first the questions you are weaker.

Or let the app shuffle all questions in random order.


Question

Question

Each question is shown with a colored category, the number, the question, and 4 options. Some questions may have pictures also.

As soon as you answer the question it will show if you have it right or wrong. A colored bar on the left also shows your result, so you can easily see which questions you got wrong.


Tags

You can easily tag a question with labels, so you can have a smart list of tagged questions.

You can use the provided labels like 'date' if the question contains a date; 'difficult' if it is a difficult question for you; 'image' if it has an image; or 'person' if it references a person.

Or you can create your own labels, and a question can have more than one tag associated with it.


Last answers

The app keeps track of your latest 5 answers. You can see a bar with the last answers you gave to a question.

The app also gives each question a rating based on the most recent answers. You can use this rating to order your questions or to use a smart list to filter, for example, the questions you got mostly wrong.


Statistics

The app gives a rating also to the exam, based on each question in it.

You can see how many questions where not answered yet, how many you got right in the last time you answered it, and how many you got right at least once.


Translate

With one click, you can share the question and its 4 options to Google Translate, which will translate to your selected language.

This is the most popular feature of the app.


Listen

Click the listen button to hear your device read the question and its 4 options to you.


Voice

You can even say the correct answer text (or letter or number) and the app will select it to you.
